About The Olympics

The History of Olympics

Since 1984 The Olympics began in ancient Greece in 776 BCE as part of religious festivals honoring Zeus. They featured athletic competitions among city-states and were held every four years. The Games were abolished in 393 CE by the Roman Empire.

In 1896, Pierre de Coubertin revived them as the present day Olympics, promoting international cohesion and sportsmanship. The Olympics have considering the fact that developed into a global occasion, symbolizing peace and excellence.
